UX Case Study

Background: Pan Am is an old American airlines that went out of business, but now is making a coming back by creating a new modern web site. Because more and more bookings are being completed online, Pan Am wants their site design to function across all digital devices.

This project required 80 hours. I conducted research, defined product requirements, explored the sites structure, created wireframes, prototypes and tested the final product. I sought participants who had experience booking airline tickets to gain insight about their experience. These users helped me understand their needs, as well as the pains and frustrations of booking airline tickets so that I could create the best user experience possible, for Pan Am Airlines. A combination of primary and secondary research paved the way for the brand new design of Pan Am Airlines first online site.

Research

First I had to create a research plan. This involved defining research goals, methods of research and targeted participants.

Primary Qualitative Research: will be used when I ask open-ended questions that lead to further insights about user's travel ticket booking experiences.

Quantitative Research: will help me determine quantifiable data like users age, gender, which device they use, locations they prefer to travel

Market Research: can help me determine what competitors are doing that works and what are modern trends.

After conducting research on participants, and synthesizing the results, I was able to come to come to some insights for Pan Am Airlines...

User Wants
Users want a system that is fast and effective at comparing prices
Users want combo package deals that save him money
Users want to travel to European countries
Users want to save time when looking for tickets
User Needs
Time to find the best deal on an airline ticket
Needs to book tickets for other members of the family
Needs to compare prices
Needs to save money
User Frustrations
Ticket prices change, go up when booking late
There is too much to fill out on online ticket booking form
There are no real-time prices
System is slow
Planning a trip involving others is stressful
Package deals make it more confusing
Emergency situations throw the user off
User Improvements
Improvements
Make system faster at comparing prices and finding the cheapest deal
Add combo deals that combine hotel and airfare
Add deals on trips to European countries
Make an online booking form that is not too lengthy to fiill out
Make site address local emergency situations
Show prices in real time
Have tracker that tells you when you should ideally book a ticket by to get a good deal

Define and Ideate

HMW Questions

These questions help us take user insights and move toward defining product requirements.

HMW 1: How might we build a feature that can quickly determine and display the cheapest airfare options?

HMW 2: How might we notify users of when they need to start booking tickets to avoid paying a higher cost?

HMW 3: How might we display trips to European countries that appeal to users?

Solution: Present travel deals on the landing page, or deals page that allow users to save money on hotel or airfare. These allinclusive package deals will include sight-seeing, hotel, airfare, tour-guides or any amenities that users can choose to include, which will save them money as costs are grouped.
Solution: Present travel deals or coupons to a wide array of popular European travel destinations like Spain, France, London, Italy, Germany, Switzerland and more.
Solution: Show beautiful appealing, high-resolution images of European travel destinations, that excite users, and link to travel deals.

Usability Test Objectives

User can select a flight
User can view a flight details
User can checkout a flight successfully
User can view other pages; My Travel Planner, Browse Deals and On Board Experience Page

Summary

All of the participants found all of the tasks easy and straightforward. Some of the participants found the limitations of the prototype confusing and needed clarification. One user needed help to find the browse deals page, and another felt that it was hard to find the flights that she should select, which required scrolling down. She felt this process should be more intuitive or flow more naturally. Most prototypes found that the prototype was good. Also, all of them thought that the flight details close button should be at the top of the pop-up because it is easier to notice, and easier and more natural to have it at the top. Next steps should be to move the flight details close button to the top and make the search results have a better user flow that is more intuitive and smoother.
