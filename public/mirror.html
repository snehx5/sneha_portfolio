UX Case Study

Mirror.com

Mirror is a long-time established retailer of family clothing. It has a style that is similar to HM, and Old Navy, carrying clothes for all age groups. As Mirror desired to break into the digital market, the company required a modern website design and brand new logo design. As the designer for this project, I was responsible for creating the best possible user experience and site design for Mirror's customers. This involved research, understanding the information architecture of the new e-commerce site, encapsulating Mirror's brand identity in the design, designing elements to mold together the user interface, and testing a tentative prototype on potential users.

1. Research

First I had to create a research plan. This involved defining research goals, methods of research and targeted participants.

Primary Qualitative Research: was used to answer questions about the users' opinions and help me get more insight regarding their online shopping experiences

Quantitative Research: was used to make sense of objective data like users' demographics and what device they most popularly use.

Quantitative and Market Research determined most popular items of users. I also explored competitors to see strengths and weaknesses of similar popular websites.

.

After conducting research on particpants, and synthesizing the results, I was able to come to come to some major insights for Mirror.com...

My Plan
Build a website that has solid copy on the landing page, and item descriptions that attract users searching for clothes online. The site shows ads, photos of a variety of clothes that are stylish, and of good quality. Website should take inspiration from JCPenney, H&M, and Amazon, stores which the users all like. Also, the website will have some sales and coupons that are not applicable in store. The UI is clean and efficiently moves the user through to the the shopping section where they can browse many clothes, and filter these based on size, and other option filters. There will be a section explaining the clothing stores system of measurements so that the user can check what size he or she is. The user can return clothes easily, like Amazon. Overall the site design is good, and saves the user time, gives them many clothing options, and makes them feel smart about their money.

5. Testing

I conducted usability tests on five people. Four of these were at Starbucks in the mall. They were able to provide feedback on the usability of the prototype by completing some tasks.

I then used an affinity map to synthesize the research into next steps.

Next Steps: The conclusions from my usability testing and affinity map provide me with a good idea of how to continue improving my prototype.
